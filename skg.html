Semantic Engineering IP

SKG · Semantic Knowledge Graph

The governed, queryable model of your business that agents stand on, across products, processes, people, and data.

The layer beneath the platformsGrounds Breeze.AI · ASIMOV · SPEX · KAPS · ECL
The premise

Agents need structured context, not retrieval guesswork

Most enterprise assistants answer by searching documents and hoping the right passage surfaces. That holds up until the question matters.

SKG, our Semantic Knowledge Graph, replaces the guesswork with structure. It is a governed, queryable model of how your business works, built for agents to consult directly. Ask a copilot a question that touches your products, your processes, or your policies, and it answers from the model your organization maintains, not from whichever paragraphs a search index returned.

SKG is the layer beneath the platforms. Assistants, copilots, and agents stand on it instead of reconstructing the business from scratch on every question.

What it is

One governed graph, four dimensions of the business

Inside, knowledge is organized as a four-layer model that agents query directly, so answers come from the model rather than from inference over fragments. Across the business, the graph spans products, processes, people, and data.

Governance

A managed asset, not a side effect

A knowledge model that anyone can edit and no one owns decays into another stale wiki. SKG is governed.

Named custodianship

Custodianship of the graph is defined: who owns the model, and who changes it.

Deliberate change

The model evolves with the business, on purpose. Updates are decisions, so agents keep answering from a version of the truth someone stands behind.
