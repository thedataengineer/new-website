Seven Modernization Archetypes, One Method

Seven delivered engagements, from COBOL on AS400 to a 3.2M line Struts estate, show one five-stage method transferring across legacy stacks.

15M+ LOC modernized10+ programs at scaleCOBOL · Delphi · VB.NET · Struts · Web Forms · Java

The first objection to any AI-led modernization proposal is familiar: our stack is too old, too odd, or too entangled for automation to work. The seven engagements below answer it with evidence. COBOL on AS400, a 3.2 million line Struts estate, a Delphi desktop product, a VB.NET monolith: different languages, different decades, all delivered with one five-stage lifecycle and an agent fleet that swaps per-language adapters instead of reinventing the method each time.

Seven engagements at a glance

The cases span insurance, healthcare and life sciences, financial services, education technology, logistics, inventory and warehouse operations, and fuel and billing. The list is representative rather than exhaustive; the same lifecycle and adapters extend to legacy stacks beyond those shown here.

  • Framework modernization, SaaS healthcare. ~3.2M LOC of Struts, Hibernate and EJB to Java Spring MVC in ~5 months.
  • Mainframe modernization, insurance. 600K LOC of COBOL on AS400 to .NET 8 microservices with a React frontend.
  • Legacy modernization, education technology. ~3M LOC of Delphi to cloud-native .NET 8 in ~8 months.
  • Monolith re-architecture, logistics. 300K LOC VB.NET monolith to modular .NET Core with React in ~5 months.
  • Version and platform upgrade, inventory and warehouse. 2.1M LOC from Java 8 to Java 21 in ~3.5 months.
  • Legacy modernization to SOA, financial services. 300K LOC of ASP.NET Web Forms to .NET Core 8 SOA with an Angular 19 frontend.
  • Monolith to microservices, fuel and billing. 825K LOC ASP.NET monolith to .NET 10 microservices with React 19.

Dead languages and scarce skills

Two of the seven were driven by business continuity. The insurance engagement moved 600K lines of COBOL on AS400 to .NET 8 microservices with a React frontend. The platform runs policy lifecycle operations for employers and employees, and every year on the mainframe deepened its exposure to a shrinking pool of COBOL skills. High-fidelity automated transformation made parallel migration streams possible across the estate, instead of a single-file rewrite queue.

The education technology case carried similar stakes. A mission-critical platform handling payments, communication and institutional workflows, holding more than 80 percent market share in Europe, ran on roughly 3 million lines of Delphi. Eight months later it was cloud-native .NET 8, moved from desktop to web, at roughly 60 percent less effort than a manual migration would have required. Faster feature delivery and a cloud-ready foundation for growth came with the exit.

Frameworks past end of life

A revenue cycle management product used by a life-sciences company to automate complex pricing, contracting and rebate processes carried about 3.2 million lines of Struts, Hibernate and EJB. A multi-staged migration with automated unit tests landed it on Java Spring MVC in about five months, with no impact on user experience during cutover and known security vulnerabilities eliminated along the way.

A lending approval platform in financial services moved 300K lines of ASP.NET Web Forms to .NET Core 8 SOA with an Angular 19 frontend over a REST API bridge layer. Two details matter to anyone who owns a compliance-sensitive system: the migration preserved undocumented data-access logic from the legacy code, and it produced AI-generated documentation for the modernized application. The institutional knowledge trapped in the old system came across, rather than dying with it.

Monoliths into modular estates

A logistics platform re-architected a 300K line VB.NET monolith into modular .NET Core with a refreshed React frontend in about five months. The payoff was higher modularity for future enhancements, better maintainability, faster enhancement cycles and easier developer onboarding. A fuel and billing platform went further: an 825K line ASP.NET monolith became .NET 10 microservices with a React 19 frontend, with AI-generated test suites reaching more than 80 percent unit coverage. Effort came in around 50 percent below a manual rewrite, transaction performance improved 20 percent, and improved usability of the modernized application produced a 15 percent productivity lift.

The overlooked archetype: version upgrades

Not every modernization changes architecture. An inventory and warehouse platform moved 2.1 million lines from Java 8 to Java 21 in about three and a half months, with automated detection and replacement of deprecated APIs. That ran roughly three times faster than a manual upgrade and doubled inventory synchronization throughput. Version upgrades rarely make roadmaps because they promise no new features; automated at this speed, the cost argument against them collapses.

What the record adds up to

Across engagements: more than 15 million lines of legacy code modernized, spanning ASP.NET, COBOL, Delphi, ASP Forms, VB.NET and custom proprietary systems. More than ten modernization programs delivered at scale across large enterprises and regulated environments, over three-plus years of field-tested method evolution. On a standalone 1 million line codebase, indicative outcomes run up to four times faster than manual modernization, with up to 70 percent migration time reduction.

Treat those figures as indicative. Actual duration depends on your path-to-production process and the modules you select for migration, and cost outcomes are framed engagement by engagement. A method with three years of field evolution should say that plainly, and this one does.

What stays constant, what changes

The stacks above share almost nothing at the code level. What they share is the method: a five-stage modernization lifecycle, an agent fleet whose per-language adapters absorb the differences between COBOL, Delphi and Web Forms, and ASIMOV as the platform that operationalizes the approach. It sits inside our Smarter Products offering, and the delivered engagements behind these archetypes are on our case studies page. When you evaluate a modernization partner, ask which of these archetypes your estate resembles and what evidence exists for that specific shape. Seven answers are above.
