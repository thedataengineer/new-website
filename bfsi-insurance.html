BFSI & Insurance

Governed agents for regulated, document-heavy financial and insurance work, across fraud, risk, and compliance.

BankingFinancial ServicesInsurance
The sector

Three tensions decide what ships in this sector

Banking, financial services, and insurance run on documents and answer to regulators. That combination determines which AI systems reach production and which stay in pilot.

Regulation
Every workflow that touches money, credit, or policyholders operates under regulatory oversight. A new system clears compliance review before it clears anything else.
Document volume
Underwriting files, claims records, loan documentation, disclosures. The core work of the sector arrives as documents, at volumes manual review cannot keep pace with.
Auditability
Regulators and internal audit ask how a result was produced, not only whether it was correct. A system that cannot show its work does not pass review.
Our approach

Governed agents: determinism and auditable evidence

BFSI and insurance are where regulated, document-heavy work fits our governed-agent approach most directly. Agents absorb the volume. Governance makes them acceptable to the people who own the risk.

Deterministic by design

A governed agent operates inside defined boundaries and follows the process it was given. The same input produces the same, reviewable behavior, so risk owners can reason about what the system will do before it does it.

Evidence, not assurances

Every action a governed agent takes leaves a trail that a reviewer, an internal auditor, or a regulator can inspect after the fact. When we say a workflow holds up under scrutiny, the audit trail is the proof.

Semantic Engineering underneath. Agents are grounded in your enterprise knowledge graph, so they act on your rules and your data instead of open-web guesswork. The governance layer is a practice of its own: Smarter Assurance covers model risk, evaluation, and the policy, provenance, and audit work that regulated deployments require.
